Collin Lucken

Hastings Postdoctoral Scholar in AI and Humanity

Bowdoin College

I am a philosopher of science investigating how agents—both human scientists and artificial systems—develop understanding through material practice. My research bridges epistemology, philosophy of mind, and the history of scientific thought, grounded in hands-on experience as a robotics engineer. I examine what it means for scientific work to count as progressive, how concepts from philosophy of mind migrate into AI research, and how the history of philosophy of science illuminates contemporary debates about knowledge and practice.

Research Areas

My work develops along three interconnected lines of inquiry. For details and publications, see the Research page.

Philosophy of Science

Scientific Progress & Engineering Epistemology

An operative account of scientific progress that foregrounds engineering, material construction, and practical cognition alongside traditional intellectual reasoning.

AI & Philosophy of Mind

Conceptual Foundations of Artificial Intelligence

How philosophical concepts—attention, understanding, thought—migrate into computational contexts, and what is lost or distorted in the transfer.

History of Philosophy of Science

Whewell, Carnap, and the Conceptual Architecture of Science

Historical work examining how past philosophers of science understood the relationship between conceptual change and empirical inquiry.
